About me

I am a fourth year PhD student in the Computer Science Department (CICS) at University of Massachusetts Amherst. I am fortunate to be advised by Arya Mazumdar. Prior to this I graduated from Indian Institute of Technology, Kharagpur in August 2016 with a Bachelors degree in Electronics and Electrical Communication Engineering.

Research

My research interests are statistical machine learning, information theory and coding theory. More concisely, I love statistical recovery problems under different settings. Under this big umbrella, I have worked on problems related to random graph theory, high dimensional integration, semi-supervised clustering, trace reconstruction and learning mixtures of distribution. Recently, I am interested in learning problems where the data is generated from a mixture of sources instead of a single source.
